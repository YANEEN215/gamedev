Knossos Board Game
The rules for this board game have been lost to time
Most mysteries on this list are tantalizing because of what they could reveal about a not-well-known civilization. But the Knossos Board Game is instead mysterious for a very different reason; no one knows how to play it.

Found by Arthur Evans during the excavations of Knossos (said to be Europe's oldest city), this object has been identified by archaeologists as an ancient board game. Made with materials like ivory, crystal, glass, gold, and silver, this object would have been as valuable in its time for its composition as it is today for its historicity.

To this day, archaeologists don't know exactly how it was played. This is such an unfortunate mystery because it must have been so much fun!
