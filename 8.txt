The Ishango Bone
This prehistoric bone could be humanity's first calendar

In the mid-20th century, archaeologists found a bone the site of Ishango in what's today the Democratic Republic of the Congo.
Dating to 20,000 years ago, this bone is more than meets the eye.

Running along the lenght of the bone in three places are series of (what appear to be) tally marks.
At one of the bone is a piece of quartz that's been attached.

Based on the pattern of marks on the bone, archaeologists and anthropologists theorize that these markings are mathematical or astronomical in nature. Others believe that this bone could be a prehistoric calendar.

Others yet believe that none of these solutions are correct at all. The bone's true significance is, unfortunately, lost to time.
