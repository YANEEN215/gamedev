The Minoan Snake Goddess
The most famous symbol of Minoan Crete is still up for interpretation

One of the most recognizable symbols of the entire Minoan Civilization is the Snake Goddess, but archaeologists know relatively little about her. Considering her fame, one would think that this object would be well understood by experts in the field. But since her discovery in 1903, scholars have been trying to figure out what and who she might be.

Dating to around 1600 BCE, this faience figurine is seldom represented in Minoan art apart from this instance. Because of this, many archaeologists have put forward various theories, from it being a representation of the Minoan "mistress of animals" deity to a woman unrelated to snakes (with the "snake" she holds instead being a cord; the sculpture was heavily restored by Arthur Evans, leading to many misinterpretations of the work).

To this day, it's unclear what this figure is supposed to represent.
