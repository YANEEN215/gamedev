Treaty Between Muwatalli II & Alaksandu
This historic treaty mentions a name familiar to those who have read The Iliad

In 1280 BCE, an ancient treaty was signed that would lead to widespread speculation over 3,000 years later. Written on a clay tablet in cuneiform, this treaty found at the ancient site of Hattusa (today Bogazköy, Turkey) would seem innocuous to most, but it's actually one of the most incredible discoveries that could alter history as we know it.

The reason is that it possibly names a figure with a legendary reputation. The two leaders named in this treaty are Muwatalli II and a warrior-king named Alaksandu of Wilusa.

We all know the ancient epic of the Trojan War, but did you know that the historic city of Troy has actually been found at an archaeological site in Turkey? It's none other than Wilusa, a city in northwestern Anatolia.

The emergency of a king named Alaksandu from this location might also ring a bell for those familiar with the Trojan War. Alaksandu is probably a transcription of the name Alexandros, which comes into English as Alexander, a famous character in Homer's epic tale.
