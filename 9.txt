The Divje Babe Flute
Could this cave bear femur be the first musical instrument ever found?

The history of music is a fascinating topic that's piqued the interest of historians and archaeologists alike. This field of study is often experimental in nature, with many trying to replicate the harmonies and sounds ancient and prehistoric humans may have made.

But were we the first to make music? An artifact uncovered in 1995 from Slovenia might prove that we weren't. This is the Divje Babe flute, found at a site occupied by Neanderthals. Carved from a cave bear femur, this object has several holes in it that appear to be like the holes of a flute.

In the decades since its discovery, this object has been hotly debated. Some say that the holes were made by an animal gnawing at the bone. Others say that the bone's holes are not consistent with animal gnawing and seem to be made by stone tools.

The lack of consensus in the scholarly community makes this object one of the most mysterious ever discovered.
