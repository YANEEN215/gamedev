Paintings Of Punt
Ancient Egyptian tomb paintings show a land geographers forgot
In the Tomb of Rekhmire on the west bank of the Nile is a bounty of paintings. As a vizier of the 18th dynasty, Rekhmire amassed enough power to have gorgeous painted displays adorning the walls of his final resting place.

This tomb is a place where a long-held mystery was resolved: the closest estimation of the Ancient Minoans' name comes from this tomb (the Keftiu). But another painting displays a mystery that archaeologists and geographers are still trying to work out.

The scene depicts a group of men carrying exotic goods, like ostrich eggs, feathers, and myrrh trees. The mystery is where these men came from. They're said to come from a land called "Punt," but the Egyptians never wrote down where this land was. Other artworks from this era also show Punt and depict the houses there being on stilts.

Today, archaeologists believe that the land of Punt may have been somewhere around the Horn of Africa, but others have even proposed the Arabian Peninsula as a possible location.
